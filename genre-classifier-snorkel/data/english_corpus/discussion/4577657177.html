You know. I agree that I don't see the need of having a man actually walk on Mars. But still I would like to see this happen...here's why.

Right now a manned mission to mars is impossible because you must have a ship large enough to store enough oxygen and fuel to make the trip to mars and back. Right now that is impossible. So, in order to complete this mission think of all the new technology that will have to be created.

First, we would have to learn of a fail-proof way of reusing the breathing air for an infinite amount of time.
Second, we would have to create or discover a new fuel source that is smaller and lighter. Plus maybe even a way of creating fuel from sometype of mineral or something that we could find on mars so we could create our own fuel to make it back to earth. Also we would need a new propulsion system.

JFK wanted to walk on the moon, but he also knew what would have to happen in order to get the job done...the same with Bush, it's not so much the footprint in Mars soil that he is looking forward to, but all the great inventions or materials that will profit not just America, but all of Mankind forever. Now that's a vision...even if we have a budget defecit for a few more years.

Ok so here I go being cynical again, but.. There is little if any comparison between JFK's 'man on the moon within the decade' goal and George's announcement.

When JFK set his manned lunar landing target, the US was involved in a very heated cold war with the Soviets. When Sputnik convinced the world that we were lagging in technology, we were frankly scared (and humiliated). There were fears that the Soviets might use their advantage for military or strategic purposes (maybe moon-based nukes sound far fetched, but this was the nervous 60's). True, there was an element of furthering knowledge but that was secondary in many ways. Oh, and the cost was somewhat more reasonable.

George's plan smells more like a campaign scheme designed to make him look more Kennedy-ish by appealing to young voters and intellectuals (the need to reach out there may be great!). Not bad for someone who previously regarded Mars and the Milky Way as candy bars. (Just kiddin)

Not that I blame him.. when you are President, you can propose all kinds of wondrous things and many other candidates would try something like that too.

Is it true he nominated Paul ONeill to helm the first mission?
