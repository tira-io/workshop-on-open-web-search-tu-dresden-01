What are MSDS's and where can I find them?

    We have moved these items to our new MSDS FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) list. You can learn what an MSDS is,what one looks like,how to read one and where to find one if you wish. Or start searching the Internet by following the links below.

Internet Resources for MSDS

General Sites

These sites have large collections of MSDS and are a good place to start your search.
    Internet Site Number of MSDS Comments
    1. MSDS Online 1,200,000 Approx 10,000 sheets are added each month. Registration and login are required to view MSDSs. OSHA, DOT, ANSI and WHIMIS compliant sheets in PDF format. Save up to 25 sheets free. Site offers a 30 day free trial of an integrated (web/software) MSDS management solution.
    2. MSDS Solutions 1,000,000 Enable cookies to search by name or 15,000 manufacturers. Registration/login is required to view the sheets.     3. Seton Compliance Resource Center 350,000 Full text searchable - Chemical name, formula, manufacturer, CAS #, symptoms of exposure, you name it! No login and no cookies required. All sheets are in 16-part ANSI compliant format as HTML files. You can also purchase this collection on CD-ROM!.
    4. MSDSs.com

    6. Vermont SIRI
    hazard.com
    (Mirror sites no longer available)    		 180,000 One of the best general sites to start a search.     7. ChemExper 100,000 Belgium. Search for chemical by name, molecular formula or CAS number. When you find the one you want click on the name of the chemical. On the resulting screen click on "MSDS". Lists suppliers as well as modeling and infrared data! Sheets in text or HTML format.
    8. CambridgeSoft web site 75,000 A growing substructure-searchable small-molecule database with 2D and 3D structures. Lots of other handy links.
    9. University of Akron 20,177 Keyword search (see search tips). Data returned are not full MSDS's,but contain useful information. HTML format.
    10. Oxford University 16,103 Chemical names listed in sections A-Z, each of which is further broken into subsections. HTML format. Optional search page is now limited to one word in name only. Some entries incomplete.
    11. Conform-Action Data Systems 5,000 Sample database that dynamically produces English or French ANSI,OSHA and WHMIS-compliant sheets. User can select HTML or PDF format. Also produces PDF labels.
    12. Hazweb 2,000+
    Down since 04-Nov    		 Must register (free; email address required). Can submit on-line requests for MSDS's not in the database. Search by keyword,name,manufacturer or CAS number. ANSI-compliant HTML format.
    13. Iowa State University 295 Typical undergraduate laboratory chemicals. Select from alphabetical list. 16-part ANSI-style sheets in HTML format.

    5a. Household Products Database
    National Library of Medicine
    (National Institutes of Health)    		 ~2,000 chemicals
    ~4,000 brands    		 For household consumers. Not MSDS's, but uses MSDS info to list/explain health effects, composition etc. of various consumer brands.     5d. The National Toxicology Program
    (National Institutes of Health)    		 228 10th annual report on known and suspected carcinogens (updated Dec 2003). Not MSDS, but great info. Also details how a chemical comes to be listed as a carcinogen. Searchable on-line or download in PDF format.
    6. New Jersey Hazardous Substance Fact Sheets (NJHSFS) 1,349 English
    298 Español    		 Right to Know site. Plain English descriptions of common household,workplace and environmental chemicals (great for non-chemists). Browse English/Spanish list. PDF format.
    7. CDC/NIOSH/WHO International Chemical Safety Cards 1,264 Select one of 13 different languages. English has U.S. and International versions. Not strictly MSDS,but close. Good for "shop floor" level of understanding. HTML format.
    8. International Agency for Research on Cancer,IARC 860 Not MSDS,but reports on cancer risks to humans by a variety of agents. Click on "SEARCH IARC Agents and Summary Evaluations",then enter chemical name or CAS number. HTML format.
    9. U.S. General Services Administration, Heartland Region 737 Select from pulldown list by facility, product name or manufacturer. Sheets for commonly used industrial, commercial ,maintenance-type items as PDF files in varying formats. Collection is meant for federal employees/contractors in MO, KS, IA and NB.
    10. NIOSH Pocket Guide to Chemical Hazards 677 Succinct tabular summaries of MSDS-type information for all materials with NIOSH exposure limits or OSHA permissible exposure limits (PEL's). Spanish version "under construction". Select from A-Z list or CAS number list. HTML format.
    11. Health Canada 173 MSDS for biohazardous agents (i.e infectious microorganisms,including anthrax = bacillus anthracis). WHMIS-compliant HTML files.
    12. Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry 109 ToxFAQ's in A-Z list. Not MSDS,but a practical Q&A approach to the health effects of chemicals. Cool molecular models and NFPA labels. HTML format.
    13. Howard Hughes Medical Institute 88 Laboratory Chemical Safety Summaries from the book,"Prudent Practices in the Laboratory: Handling and Disposal of Chemicals". Very readable although not strictly MSDS's. HTML format.
    14. National Safety Council
    Chemical Backgrounders    		 82 Gives chemical descriptions, properties,health effects,references and economics,but is not an MSDS. HTML format.
    15. U.S. Environmental Protection Agency 31 Fact sheets in text format,a few in PDF.
    16. Gulf War Veterans Resource Pages 6 Scroll down to Appendix A for links to military unique MSDS on Mustard (HD, THD),Tabun (GA),Sarin (GB), Soman (GD), VX (nerve agent) and Lewisite (HT) - hopefully you won't need to use these! HTML files in OSHA-compliant format.
